---
title: "Link Resident Council grievances"
description: "Record which facility meeting produced a Resident Council grievance and navigate the relationship from either record."
---

A Resident Council grievance can optionally link to the meeting where the concern was raised. The link preserves source context and appears on both the grievance and meeting records.

Existing Resident Council grievances remain valid without a meeting link.

## Create and link a grievance

1. Open the **New grievance** composer.
2. Confirm the facility.
3. Select **Submitted at Resident Council Meeting**.
4. Select **Link meeting**.
5. Search or browse same-facility meetings. Use **View** to review a meeting's details, notes, and linked grievances before selecting it.
6. Choose **Select meeting**, finish the concern and routing details, and create the grievance.

The meeting link is optional. If the meeting has not been entered yet, create the grievance without a link and add it later.

<Note>
  A Resident Council submission is treated as meeting-derived rather than resident-specific. CareResolve omits resident and reporter identity from that intake record while retaining the signed-in team member as the filer.
</Note>

## Link or change an existing grievance

Only an unlocked internal intake record can be changed.

1. Open the grievance.
2. Open **Intake record** from the timeline.
3. Select the pencil action.
4. Select **Submitted at Resident Council Meeting** if it is not already selected.
5. Link, change, or remove the meeting.
6. Save the intake record.

Clearing the Resident Council option also removes the source-meeting link. Public intake packets, pending-approval cases, and resolved cases remain read-only through this editor.

## Where the link appears

- The grievance right rail shows a **Resident Council meeting** section with the meeting title, date, and time.
- The meeting page shows the grievance under **Linked grievances** with its current status.
- The grievance log's **Submitted at Resident Council** filter includes Council submissions whether or not a meeting has been linked.

Select either relationship to open the other record.

## Source link versus note reference

These two meeting connections serve different purposes:

| Connection | Where it is created | What it means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Source meeting | Grievance intake record | The concern was submitted at that Resident Council meeting |
| `#` grievance reference | Meeting notes | The notes mention or discuss that grievance |

A `#` reference in meeting notes does not automatically make the meeting the grievance's source. Add the source link from the grievance when provenance matters.

## Facility and deletion rules

The grievance and meeting must belong to the same facility. Changing facility scope or search terms cannot expose meetings outside your access.

An administrator cannot delete a meeting while source-linked grievances remain. Open each linked grievance, remove or change its source meeting, and then retry deletion.
