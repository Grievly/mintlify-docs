---
title: "Resolve a grievance"
description: "Document findings, actions, notification, and resolution details."
---

Resolve a grievance when the investigation, follow-up, and required communication are complete.

Some facilities require resolution approvals before a grievance can be marked resolved. In those facilities, the resolution action becomes **Submit for approval** instead of direct **Resolve**.

## Before resolving

Confirm that the record includes:

- The concern summary
- Follow-up notes or actions taken
- Investigation findings
- Actions taken or planned
- Resident or representative communication, when applicable
- Attachments or related records needed to support the outcome
- Any required labels, assignee, and facility information

## Complete the resolution

If the facility does not require resolution approvals:

1. Open the grievance.
2. Go to the resolution section.
3. Enter the investigation summary, findings, and actions taken.
4. Add the **Outcome date** and an optional resolved note.
5. Record who received the report-back and when. Anonymous reports do not require a recipient.
6. Save the draft when you want to return later.
7. Select **Resolve** and confirm.

After resolution, the grievance becomes read-only in most areas. This protects the final case record.

The operator-entered **Outcome date** records when the underlying outcome was completed. It is separate from the system-recorded finalization time shown in the resolved grievance list, which may be later when approvals are required.

## Submit for approval

If the facility requires resolution approvals:

1. Complete the same resolution fields.
2. Select **Submit for approval**.
3. Confirm the submission.

The grievance stays open while approval is pending. Required signers review the resolution details and sign from the approval panel on the grievance.

If you are the only required signer for the facility, Grievly shows **Resolve grievance** and explains that no additional approvals are required.

## Reopen if needed

If new information arrives after resolution, any user who can access the grievance can reopen it and continue the timeline.

Choose a reason for reopening. If you choose **Other**, additional notes are required. The reason appears on the timeline.

Reopening keeps the original history and due date and allows additional work to be documented.

If the grievance was resolved through approvals, reopening clears the active approval state. The reopened grievance must be resolved or submitted again under the facility's current settings.

## Draft with AI

Select **Draft with AI** to populate an editable resolution draft from the grievance record and case notes.

- Grievly warns before overwriting existing resolution content, including unsaved changes.
- The draft can flag fields that need better support from the case record.
- Review and edit every field before saving or submitting.
- The AI draft never resolves the grievance or sends an approval request on its own.

## Improve individual fields

Resolution text fields also include an **Improve writing** sparkle control. The suggested wording temporarily replaces the draft so you can accept it or dismiss it and restore the original.

The wording is not persisted until you save the resolution or submit it through the normal workflow.

## Review against federal guidance

After an F-tagged grievance is resolved, Grievly automatically prepares a collapsed **Compliance review** the first time the resolved case is viewed. The alignment result remains visible in the header.

The review compares the documented resolution with the selected F-tag's federal Appendix PP guidance. Expand it to see the short assessment and any gaps to consider.

This review is advisory and federal-only. It does not change the resolution, reopen the grievance, determine compliance, or account for state-specific requirements.

## Resolution quality

A clear resolution should answer what the concern was, what the facility reviewed, what the facility found, what action was taken or planned, and who was notified. This is especially important when records are reviewed later by leadership, QAPI, or survey teams.
