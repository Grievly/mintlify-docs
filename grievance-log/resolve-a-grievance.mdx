Resolve a grievance when investigation, follow-up, and required communication are complete.

## Before resolving

Confirm the case contains the concern, investigation notes, findings, actions taken or planned, report-back details when applicable, supporting attachments, and the correct labels and assignee.

## Complete the resolution

1. Open the resolution section.
2. Enter the investigation summary, findings, and actions taken.
3. Add the **Outcome date** and an optional resolved note.
4. Record who received the report-back and when. Anonymous reports do not require a recipient.
5. Save a draft if you need to return later.
6. Select **Resolve** and confirm.

The Outcome date records when the underlying outcome was completed. It can differ from the system-recorded finalization time, especially when approvals delay completion.

If the facility requires sign-off, the action is **Submit for approval**. The grievance stays open and locked while required signers review it. A submitter who is the only required signer can resolve it in the same confirmation flow.

## Draft and improve writing

**Draft with AI** creates editable resolution text from the grievance and case notes. Grievly warns before overwriting existing content and can flag fields that need stronger support. Review every field; AI never resolves the grievance or submits an approval request.

Use **Improve writing** on an individual field to accept or dismiss a wording suggestion. Nothing is persisted until you save or submit through the normal workflow.

## After resolution

Most of the existing case becomes read-only to protect the final record. For an F-tagged grievance, Grievly prepares a collapsed advisory **Compliance review** that compares the documented resolution with federal Appendix PP guidance.

If new information arrives, any user with case access can reopen the grievance. Choose a reason; **Other** also requires notes. Reopening keeps history and the original due date, clears active approval state, and returns the case to the open workflow.
