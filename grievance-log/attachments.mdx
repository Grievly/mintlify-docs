---
title: "Attachments"
description: "Upload, review, and manage supporting files on a grievance."
---

Attachments are supporting files that belong with a grievance record.

Use attachments for documents, images, or other files the facility needs to review or retain with the case.

## Add an attachment

1. Open the grievance.
2. Find **Attachments** in the right rail.
3. Upload the file.
4. Wait for upload and scanning to complete.

Attachments also appear in the case timeline so reviewers can see when files were added.

## Scan status

Uploaded files go through security scanning before they can be opened.

Common states include:

- **Pending**: scan is queued.
- **Scanning**: scan is running.
- **Clean**: file can be opened.
- **Flagged**: scan found a concern.
- **Failed**: scan did not complete.

If a file is flagged or failed, do not rely on it until the issue is reviewed.

## AI analysis

Some attachments may also be analyzed for case context when supported.

AI attachment analysis is advisory. It can help summarize or surface context, but the facility remains responsible for reviewing the actual file and documenting the case.

## Delete an attachment

Open the attachment action menu and choose delete.

Deletion removes the attachment from the active case view, but the activity remains part of the audit trail.

## Attachment safety

Only upload files that belong with the grievance.

Reports with attachments may contain sensitive resident, reporter, or case information. Share them only with approved people.
