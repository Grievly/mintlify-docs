description: "Use mentions, grievance references, subscriptions, and notifications to keep teammates informed."

Use case-note mentions and subscriptions when someone needs attention on a grievance or ongoing updates.

## Mention a teammate

1. Start a case note.
2. Type `@` and choose the teammate.
3. Finish and add the note.

The teammate receives an in-app notification and, when email is available, an email. They are also subscribed to future case activity.

<Note>
  A mention does not grant access. The teammate still needs access to the organization and facility.
</Note>

## Reference another grievance

Type `#` in a case note and choose the related grievance. The reference becomes a link, and the referenced grievance receives a timeline event pointing back to the current case.

Use references for repeat concerns, related submissions, follow-up cases, or records leadership should review together.

## Subscribe to a case

Use **Notifications** in the grievance right rail to subscribe or unsubscribe. A subscription can notify you when someone else adds a note, changes assignment or status, updates labels, adds an attachment, changes the resolution, or references the grievance.

Grievly automatically subscribes active participants, including the creator, assignee, note authors, and mentioned teammates. If you unsubscribe and later participate again, Grievly may subscribe you again.

Editing an existing case note does not send another activity notification. Grievly also avoids emailing you about an action you performed yourself.

## Use the notification bell

The bell shows activity from the last 30 days. From the panel you can:

- Show unread notifications only.
- Open the related grievance, report, or destination.
- Mark one notification read or unread.
- Mark all notifications read.
- Open **Manage notifications**.

Opening a notification with a destination marks it read. Case-note notifications open at the referenced note when available. The unread badge is capped at **99+**.

## Choose email preferences

Select your avatar, choose **Account settings**, and open **Notifications**. Choose facilities for new-grievance email and whether to receive assignment or overdue-grievance email.

Administrators can manage the same choices for a teammate from **Team > user > Notifications**.

## Keep the case as the source of truth

Email notifications identify the grievance, facility, action, and link back to Grievly. They omit case-note text and full grievance narratives. Review the complete case in Grievly rather than treating an email as the record.
