Create a grievance whenever a concern needs a trackable record, an owner, follow-up, or documented resolution.

## Create the record

1. Select **Create** in the top bar.
2. Confirm the facility and choose an assignee or departments to notify when needed.
3. Add the resident, unit, room or area, and occurrence details that are known.
4. Record employee or third-party involvement when applicable.
5. Enter the concern in clear, specific language.
6. Identify the reporter, mark the report anonymous, or select **Resident reported this concern**.
7. Select **Create**.

CareResolve opens the new case after saving it.

## Capture what the team needs

Include enough information for someone else to begin the review:

- Who raised the concern and which resident is involved.
- What happened, when, and where.
- Who should follow up.
- Any immediate safety or escalation need.

Use the resident's words when they matter, but avoid unrelated sensitive details.

## Reporter choices

Select **Resident reported this concern** only when the resident personally raised it. CareResolve copies the resident name into the reporter section; contact details can still be added.

For anonymous reports, preserve anonymity while recording enough operational detail to investigate safely. Do not use the resident option for a family member, representative, ombudsman, staff member, or other third party.

## Routing and notifications

- **Facility** controls access, reporting, locations, and public-intake source.
- **Assignee** identifies the person who owns the next step.
- **Departments to Notify** emails users assigned to the selected facility departments.

Departments supplement the assignee; they do not replace case ownership.

## Draft behavior and writing help

On desktop, you can minimize the composer or collapse it into a draft bar. On mobile, it opens full screen. CareResolve warns before discarding an unfinished draft and summarizes missing required fields when you try to create an incomplete record.

The sparkle control can suggest clearer concern wording. Review and accept or dismiss the suggestion; it never saves the grievance for you.

After intake, CareResolve may suggest a title, summary, category, F-tag, and High Priority marker. Staff remain responsible for review and follow-up.
