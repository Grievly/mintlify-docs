---
title: "Create a grievance"
description: "Create a new grievance, complaint, or resident concern in Grievly."
---

Create a grievance when a resident, family member, representative, staff member, or other reporter raises a concern that needs review, response, or documented follow-up.

Admins can create grievances for any active facility. Members can create grievances for facilities they can access.

## Create the record

1. Open **Grievances**.
2. Select **New grievance**.
3. Choose the facility.
4. Add resident information, including unit or room when available.
5. Add reporter information, mark the report anonymous, or choose **Resident reported this concern** when the resident is also the reporter.
6. Enter the grievance, complaint, or concern details.
7. Add timing, location, employee involvement, and other incident details when known.
8. Choose routing details such as assignee and departments to notify.
9. Save the grievance.

## What to include

Capture enough information for another team member to understand the issue without reconstructing it later.

- Who raised the concern
- Which resident is involved
- What happened
- When and where it happened
- Who should follow up
- Any immediate safety or escalation needs

Use the resident's words when they matter to the review, but avoid adding unnecessary sensitive details.

## Routing details

Routing fields help the team work the case after intake.

- **Facility** controls visibility, reporting, public intake source, and available locations.
- **Departments to Notify** sends email notifications to members assigned to selected facility departments.
- **Assignee** identifies who owns the next step.
- **Category** gives the case an operational bucket after intake analysis and can be confirmed from the case metadata rail.

Grievly may also suggest a title, summary, F-tag, category, and High Priority marker after intake analysis.

## Resident as reporter

Use **Resident reported this concern** when the resident is the person raising the grievance.

When this is selected:

- The reporter name is copied from the resident name.
- Reporter relationship is kept read-only.
- Reporter email and phone can still be added when the resident provided contact information.
- The case remains a resident grievance, not an anonymous report.

Do not use this option when a family member, representative, ombudsman, staff member, or other third party reported on the resident's behalf.

## Department notifications

Use **Departments to Notify** when a facility department should be alerted as soon as the grievance is created.

Only departments configured for the selected facility appear. A department notification emails users assigned to that department.

Departments are not a replacement for the case assignee. Use the assignee field for the person who owns the next step.

## Use consistent language

People may call the same event a grievance, complaint, concern, issue, report, request, or family concern. In Grievly, log it when the facility needs a trackable record, ownership, follow-up, or resolution documentation.

## Anonymous reports

If the reporter is anonymous, preserve that choice and avoid adding identifying details unless they are necessary for safe follow-up.

Anonymous reports can still include enough operational detail for the facility to investigate safely.

## Correct intake details later

If an open grievance was saved with the wrong resident, reporter, timing, or concern details, use the original submission editor from the case timeline.
