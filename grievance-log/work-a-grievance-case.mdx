title: "Work a grievance case"
description: "Use the grievance detail page to review, update, add case notes to, and resolve a case."
---

The grievance detail page is the working record for a case.

It has three main areas:

- **Header** for title, display ID, status, High Priority, and case actions.
- **Timeline** for the intake record, AI summary, case notes, activity, attachments, and resolution prompts.
- **Right rail** for notifications, case metadata, pattern membership, attachments, and participants.

Breadcrumbs and page context appear inside the page content so the working case record stays close to the top of the detail page.

## Case anatomy

| Area | What it contains |
| --- | --- |
| Header | Title, grievance ID, status, High Priority, and case actions |
| Timeline | Intake record, AI summary, case notes, activity, attachments, and resolution prompts |
| Right rail | Notifications, metadata, patterns, attachments, and participants |

## Header

The header shows the case title, grievance ID, current status, and created or resolved context.

Use the three-dot action menu to print a summary or edit the title on an unlocked grievance.

Administrators can also delete an unresolved grievance. Resolved grievances cannot be deleted.

## Timeline

Use the timeline to understand what happened and what has changed.

The timeline can include:

- The opening submission item with an **Intake record** link.
- AI summary and suggested labels.
- System events such as assignment, category, F-tag, status, attachment, and resolution changes.
- Case notes.
- Attachment entries.
- Resolution call-to-action when the case is still open.
- Resolution approval panels when the facility requires sign-off before completion.

## Intake record

Select **Intake record** from the opening timeline item to review the full source packet.

Internal intake records open in view mode. On an unlocked grievance, select the pencil icon to correct details such as facility, resident or reporter information, occurrence date, employee involvement, or the original concern text.

Public intake records are always read-only so the submitter's original intent is preserved. Resolved grievances and grievances pending resolution approval are also locked.

## Case notes

Use case notes for investigation updates, follow-up, review notes, and coordination.

Use `@` to mention a teammate. Use `#` to reference another grievance.

On an unlocked grievance, a case-note author can edit or delete their own note. Edited notes are marked as edited.

The sparkle control can improve the wording of a draft note. Accept or dismiss the suggestion, then save the note normally; AI never saves it for you.

Case notes may contain sensitive case information. Keep the full record in Grievly and avoid copying PHI into outside tools.

## Right rail

The right rail keeps the most important reference information visible while you work the case.

| Rail panel | Use it for |
| --- | --- |
| Notifications | Subscribe or unsubscribe from future activity |
| Case metadata | Review assignee, status and due date, resident, reporter, involved employees, F-tag, and category |
| Pattern membership | Open related patterns when the grievance is part of one |
| Attachments | Upload and review supporting files |
| Participants | See people who have touched the case |

### Notifications

Subscribe to receive future case activity notifications. Unsubscribe when you no longer need updates.

### Case metadata

Review assignee, status and due date, resident, reporter details, involved employees, F-tag, and category.

Assignee, category, and F-tag can be edited while the grievance is open and not pending approval.

### Pattern membership

If the grievance belongs to a pattern, select it to open the pattern details over the case. A grievance can belong to more than one pattern.

### Attachments

Upload and review supporting files from the rail. Attachments can also appear in the timeline.

Timeline attachment cards show scan state, Open and Download actions when the file is available, and an AI summary panel when supported.

If an attachment may not match the concern, Grievly shows a warning and keeps the full AI summary collapsed until you expand it.

### Participants

Participants summarize people who have touched the case, such as case-note authors, assignees, attachment uploaders, subscribers, or people involved in status changes.

## Resolved cases

After a grievance is resolved, existing notes, attachments, intake details, labels, assignment, and resolution fields are read-only.

Administrators can append a new case note or attachment without reopening the grievance. The new item becomes part of the post-resolution record, but existing notes and attachments remain read-only.

Any user who can access the grievance can reopen it when more work is needed. Choose a reason and add context when useful; the reason appears on the timeline. Reopening preserves the original due date and history.

## Pending resolution approval

Some facilities require selected signers to approve a resolution before the grievance is completed.

When a grievance is pending approval, the timeline shows required signers and their signed or pending state. Resolution fields, existing case notes, and existing attachments are read-only.

Users with case access can add a new attachment while approval is pending. Administrators can also append a case note. Cancel the approval request before changing the resolution or editing existing case content.
