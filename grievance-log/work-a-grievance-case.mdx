title: "Work a grievance case"
description: "Use the grievance detail page to review, update, comment on, and resolve a case."
---

The grievance detail page is the working record for a case.

It has three main areas:

- **Header** for title, display ID, status, High Priority, and reopen actions.
- **Timeline** for intake, AI summary, comments, activity, attachments, and resolution prompts.
- **Right rail** for notifications, case metadata, pattern membership, attachments, and participants.

## Case anatomy

| Area | What it contains |
| --- | --- |
| Header | Title, grievance ID, status, High Priority, and reopen action |
| Timeline | Intake, AI summary, comments, activity, attachments, and resolution prompts |
| Right rail | Notifications, metadata, patterns, attachments, and participants |

## Header

The header shows the case title, grievance ID, current status, and created or resolved context.

Open grievances can have their title edited. Resolved grievances are read-only in most places.

Admins can reopen resolved grievances.

## Timeline

Use the timeline to understand what happened and what has changed.

The timeline can include:

- Intake information.
- AI summary and suggested labels.
- System events such as assignment, category, F-tag, status, attachment, and resolution changes.
- Comments.
- Attachment entries.
- Resolution call-to-action when the case is still open.

## Comments

Use comments for follow-up notes, investigation updates, review notes, and coordination.

Use `@` to mention a teammate. Use `#` to reference another grievance.

Comments may contain sensitive case information. Keep the full case record in Grievly and avoid copying PHI into outside tools.

## Right rail

The right rail keeps the most important reference information visible while you work the case.

| Rail panel | Use it for |
| --- | --- |
| Notifications | Subscribe or unsubscribe from future activity |
| Case metadata | Review source, due date, resident, reporter, assignee, F-tag, and category |
| Pattern membership | Open related patterns when the grievance is part of one |
| Attachments | Upload and review supporting files |
| Participants | See people who have touched the case |

### Notifications

Subscribe to receive future case activity notifications. Unsubscribe when you no longer need updates.

### Case metadata

Review facility, status, due date, source, filed date, resident, reporter, assignee, F-tag, and category.

Assignee and category can be edited while the grievance is open. F-tag override is admin-only.

### Pattern membership

If the grievance belongs to a pattern, the rail links to that pattern. Patterns are clusters of similar grievances found by Grievly.

### Attachments

Upload and review supporting files from the rail. Attachments can also appear in the timeline.

### Participants

Participants summarize people who have touched the case, such as commenters, assignees, or people involved in status changes.

## Resolved cases

After a grievance is resolved, most editing controls are disabled. This preserves the case record.

If new information arrives, an admin can reopen the grievance and continue the timeline.
