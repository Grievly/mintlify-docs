---
title: "Edit an internal intake record"
description: "Correct an internal intake packet while preserving the case timeline and public submissions."
---

The **Intake record** shows the source packet that opened a grievance. It starts in view mode so staff can review the captured information before making changes.

Only internally filed intake records can be edited. Public submissions remain read-only to preserve the submitter's original intent.

## What you can edit

On an unlocked internal grievance, editable fields can include:

- Facility.
- Resident name, room, and unit.
- Occurrence date and shift.
- Resident Council submission.
- Employee involvement and involved employee names.
- Outside-party involvement.
- Concern details.
- Anonymous-report status.
- Reporter name, relationship, email, and phone.

The assignee, category, and F-tag are managed from the case metadata rail. The title is managed from the grievance action menu.

## Edit the intake record

1. Open the grievance.
2. Select **Intake record** from the opening timeline item.
3. Select the pencil icon in the upper-right.
4. Correct the fields.
5. Review the **Unsaved changes** indicator.
6. Select **Save changes**.

If no fields changed, the save action remains disabled.

The concern field includes an **Improve writing** control. Review and accept or dismiss the suggested wording before saving. The suggestion does not persist on its own.

## Public submissions

Public intake records do not show the edit control. The dialog explains that the source packet is preserved.

If later investigation clarifies or contradicts a public submission, add a case note describing what was learned. Use the assignee and label controls to route the case without rewriting the original report.

## Locked internal records

An internal intake record is locked while its grievance is resolved or pending resolution approval.

If more work is needed after resolution, reopen the grievance, choose a reason, and then make the correction. Reopening preserves the existing case history and due date.

## What happens after saving

Grievly updates the internal intake record and adds timeline activity for changed fields. The edit does not remove case notes, attachments, resolution documentation, or earlier activity.
