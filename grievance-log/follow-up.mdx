Follow-up is documented through case notes and attachments on the grievance timeline.

For the full workflow, see [Work a grievance case](/grievance-log/work-a-grievance-case), [Mentions and notifications](/grievance-log/mentions-and-notifications), and [Attachments](/grievance-log/attachments).

## Add a case note

1. Open the grievance.
2. Find **Add case note** below the timeline.
3. Document what you reviewed, who you spoke with, and what you found.
4. Select **Add note**.

Use the sparkle control to request clearer wording. Accept or dismiss the suggestion before adding the note.

On an unlocked grievance, only the author can edit or delete an existing case note. Edited notes are identified on the timeline.

## Mention teammates

Use `@` when a teammate needs to review or take part in the case. Select the person from the suggestion list before saving.

The teammate receives a notification and is subscribed to future grievance activity.

## Reference another grievance

Use `#` to link a related grievance from the same facility, such as a repeat concern or earlier report.

The reference becomes a link in the case note, and the referenced grievance receives timeline activity showing how the cases are connected.

## Document meaningful actions

Case notes can record steps such as:

- Speaking with a resident or representative.
- Reviewing a chart or internal record.
- Interviewing staff.
- Escalating to leadership.
- Assigning corrective action.
- Completing follow-up.

Write specific, dated notes. Avoid vague updates such as "handled" when the record needs to show what was reviewed, who was contacted, and what changed.

## Add attachments

Use attachments for supporting files that belong with the grievance. Do not upload unrelated files or copy case material into an unapproved system.

## Locked cases

Existing case notes and attachments become read-only while a resolution approval is pending or after the grievance is resolved.

Administrators can append a case note or attachment after resolution. While approval is pending, administrators can append a case note and users with case access can upload an attachment.
