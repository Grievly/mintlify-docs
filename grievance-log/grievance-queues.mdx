---
title: "Grievance queues"
description: "Use All grievances, My queue, Overdue, and High Priority to find work that needs attention."
---

The sidebar includes queue shortcuts for the grievance log. Each shortcut opens the same grievance list with different filters applied.

## All grievances

Use **All grievances** to search and filter the full grievance log you can access.

Admins see organization-wide work. Members see grievances for their assigned facilities.

## My queue

Use **My queue** for grievances assigned to you.

This is the fastest place to review personal follow-up work, unresolved assignments, cases waiting on your documentation, and resolutions waiting for your approval.

If you are a required signer for a pending resolution approval, the grievance appears in **My queue** even when you are not the assignee.

## Overdue

Use **Overdue** for open grievances past their SLA deadline.

Overdue is derived from the due date on an open grievance. It is not a separate saved status. When a grievance is resolved, it no longer appears in the Overdue queue.

## High Priority

Use **High Priority** for open grievances marked for faster operator attention.

High Priority is an operational marker created during AI intake analysis when serious trigger language is detected. It is not a legal finding, citation, or state-reporting decision.

Resolved grievances no longer appear in the High Priority queue.

## Queue counts

Sidebar counts help you spot work volume quickly. Counts respect your role and facility access.

If a count looks different for two users, confirm whether they have the same role and facility scope.

## Pending approval

**Pending approval** is a list filter for grievances that have been submitted for resolution approval but are not yet resolved.

Pending approval grievances remain open until every required signer approves or an administrator bypasses remaining approvals.
