---
title: "Review public submissions"
description: "Review, route, and work grievances submitted through a facility's public intake form."
---

Public intake submissions create grievance records for facility review. The submitter sees a thank-you confirmation, but Grievly does not expose the internal grievance number on that public screen.

## What submitters can provide

The public form can capture:

- Submitter type: anonymous, employee, family member, or resident.
- Reporter name, relationship, email, and phone when applicable.
- Resident name, room, or unit when provided.
- Occurrence date.
- Employee involvement and involved employee names.
- A concern narrative.

## Review the record

1. Open the new grievance.
2. Confirm that the opening timeline item says **Submitted via public form**.
3. Select **Intake record** and review the full source packet.
4. Check the resident, reporter, and anonymous-report details.
5. Review the AI summary and suggested labels when available.
6. Confirm the assignee, category, and F-tag.
7. Add a case note for clarifications or investigation findings.
8. Work the case from the timeline.

The grievance list also uses a public-intake QR badge to distinguish these records from internally filed grievances.

## Preserve the intake record

Public intake records cannot be edited. This preserves the submitter's original intent and source record.

If the team later confirms a correction or receives more detail, document it in a case note. You can still change operational fields such as the title, assignee, category, and F-tag while the grievance is unlocked.

## Anonymous reports

If the submitter chose anonymous, preserve that choice. Do not add identifying details unless they are necessary for safe follow-up and are supported by the record.

## Continue the workflow

Public grievances use the same case-note, attachment, assignment, label, resolution, and approval workflows as internal grievances. The immutable intake packet remains visible throughout the case.
