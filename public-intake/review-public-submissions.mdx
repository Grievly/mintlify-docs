---
title: "Review public submissions"
description: "Review, route, and work grievances submitted through public intake."
---

Public intake submissions create grievance records for facility review.

They should be reviewed before the team relies on routing details or begins follow-up.

## What submitters can provide

The public intake form can capture:

- Submitter type: anonymous, employee, family member, or resident.
- Resident name, room, or unit when provided.
- Reporter contact information when not anonymous.
- Concern summary.
- Resident words.
- Approximate timing.
- Shift.
- Employee or vendor involvement.
- Other involved people.

## Review the record

1. Open the new grievance.
2. Confirm the facility and source.
3. Review resident and reporter details.
4. Check whether the report is anonymous.
5. Review the AI summary and suggested labels when available.
6. Confirm assignee, department, category, and F-tag.
7. Work the case from the timeline.

## Anonymous reports

If the submitter chose anonymous, preserve that choice.

Do not add identifying details unless they are necessary for safe follow-up and are supported by the record.

## Public source label

Public submissions are labeled as public source records in the case metadata rail.

This helps reviewers distinguish external intake from internally created grievances.
