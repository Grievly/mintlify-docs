Facilities control where grievances are assigned, filtered, reported, and reviewed.

Grievly provisions facilities and maintains their profile and activation status. In the product app, administrators configure day-to-day facility operations; members can review information for facilities they can access.

## Find a facility

1. Open **Facilities**.
2. Search by facility name, location, or CMS provider number when needed.
3. Select a facility to open it.

Only active facilities appear in this list. Contact your Grievly account manager to add a facility, change its profile, or change its activation status.

## Why facilities matter

Facilities are used throughout Grievly for:

- Grievance routing and visibility
- Dashboard and analytics scope
- Reports
- Public intake links
- Team access
- Locations and departments
- Patterns

## Facility tabs

Each facility has four tabs: **Details**, **Locations**, **Departments**, and **Configuration**.

| Tab | What it contains | Who can change it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Details | Facility facts, resolution approvals, and public intake | Admins configure approvals; the facility profile is read-only |
| Locations | Units, rooms, and areas | Admins |
| Departments | Routing groups and assigned members | Admins |
| Configuration | Default grievance SLA | Admins |

Members can review these tabs for facilities they can access.

### Details

The facility profile shows the city, state, bed count, and CMS provider number. These fields are read-only in the product app.

Administrators can configure **Resolution approvals** on this tab:

1. Turn on **Require resolution approvals**.
2. Select one or more required signers.
3. Save the setting.

Only people with access to the facility can be selected. Organization admins are also eligible because they can access every facility. The signer list is captured when a resolution is submitted, so later setting changes apply to new requests.

<Note>
  Approvals cannot be enabled without at least one required signer. If approvals are off, the facility uses direct resolution.
</Note>

The **Public intake** panel shows the permanent intake URL, QR code, last-used time, and download actions. Users with access to the facility can copy the URL or download a poster or QR PNG.

### Locations

Locations are reference data for units, rooms, common areas, and other facility locations. They populate intake fields while each grievance keeps its own point-in-time resident room and unit values.

Administrators can:

- Create a unit, room, or standalone area.
- Search and rename locations.
- Move rooms or areas between units.
- Bulk create, move, or delete locations.

Review changes carefully when staff rely on these names for consistent intake.

### Departments

Departments are facility-specific routing groups such as Nursing, Dietary, Maintenance, Social Services, or Administration.

Administrators can create departments and assign members. A member must already have access to the facility before being assigned to one of its departments. Department membership can affect intake routing and notifications.

### Configuration

The **Grievance SLA** controls the due-date window for new grievances at the facility. Administrators enter the value in hours.

Changing the SLA affects new grievances only. Existing due dates and timeline history are not rewritten.

## If a facility is missing

First confirm that you are in the correct organization. Members should ask an administrator to confirm their facility access. If the facility has not been provisioned or activated, contact your Grievly account manager.
