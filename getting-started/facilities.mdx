Facilities control where grievances are assigned, filtered, reported, and reviewed.

Only administrators can create, edit, archive, restore, or configure facilities.

Set up facilities before inviting most users or sharing intake links. This keeps grievance records tied to the correct location from the start.

## Add a facility

1. Open **Facilities** from the sidebar.
2. Select **New facility**.
3. Enter the facility name and required details.
4. Save the facility.

## Edit a facility

1. Open **Facilities**.
2. Select the facility.
3. Choose **Edit**.
4. Update the details and save.

## Why facilities matter

Facilities are used throughout Grievly for:

- Grievance routing
- Dashboard filters
- Reports
- Public intake links
- Team access and visibility
- Locations and departments
- Patterns and analytics

## Facility tabs

Each facility has four tabs: **Details**, **Locations**, **Departments**, and **Configuration**.

| Tab | Main job | Admin-only actions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Details | Facility facts, resolution approvals, and public intake | Edit details, configure required signers, copy link, download QR poster |
| Locations | Units, rooms, and areas | Create, rename, move, bulk create, bulk delete |
| Departments | Routing groups | Create, edit, assign members, delete |
| Configuration | Facility lifecycle | Archive or restore |

### Details

Use **Details** to review or update the facility name, city, state, bed count, and CMS provider number.

Administrators can also configure **Resolution approvals** on this tab.

Resolution approvals let a facility require specific people to sign off before a grievance resolution is completed. When enabled, users submit the resolution for approval instead of resolving directly. Every required signer captured at submission time must approve, unless an administrator explicitly bypasses remaining approvals.

To configure approvals:

1. Turn on **Require resolution approvals**.
2. Select one or more required signers.
3. Save the setting.

Only users with access to the facility can be selected as signers. Organization admins can also be selected.

<Note>
  Approvals cannot be enabled without at least one required signer. If approvals are off, the facility keeps the standard direct resolution workflow.
</Note>

The public intake panel is also on this tab for administrators. It shows the permanent intake URL, QR code, last-used information, and poster download.

Members can view basic facility details when they have access, but administrator access is required to manage details and public intake links.

### Locations

Use **Locations** to manage reference data for units, rooms, and areas.

Locations populate intake and grievance location fields. They do not replace the point-in-time resident room and unit snapshots kept on each grievance.

Common location work:

- Create a unit.
- Create rooms or areas inside a unit.
- Create standalone rooms or areas.
- Search locations.
- Move rooms or areas between units.
- Rename locations.
- Bulk create rooms.
- Bulk move or delete locations.

Do not delete or rename locations casually if staff rely on them for consistent intake.

### Departments

Use **Departments** to create facility-specific routing groups.

Departments can have assigned members. A member must have access to the facility before they can be assigned to one of its departments. Admins can be selected because admins have organization-wide access.

Departments are useful for routing work to Nursing, Dietary, Maintenance, Social Services, Administration, or other facility teams.

### Configuration

Use **Configuration** to archive or restore a facility.

Archiving keeps historical records available, but removes the facility from new intake, public intake, and new grievance assignment until it is restored.

Restoring makes the facility available again for new work.

## Naming facilities

Use the facility name your team recognizes in daily work and reporting. If the legal name differs from the operating name, choose the name that will make the grievance log easiest for staff to search and review.
