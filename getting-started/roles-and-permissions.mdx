---
title: "Roles and permissions"
description: "Understand what administrators and members can see and manage in Grievly."
---

Grievly uses two workspace roles: **Admin** and **Member**.

Admins manage the workspace. Members work within the facilities they have been given access to.

## Role overview

| Area | Admin | Member |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Facility visibility | All active facilities in the organization | Assigned facilities only |
| Team | Invite admins or members, edit access, manage notifications, revoke invites, and remove users | Invite members within accessible facilities and view the scoped directory |
| Facilities | Configure organization-wide facility operations | View accessible facility context and public intake links |
| Grievances | Work organization-wide grievances | Work accessible facility grievances |
| F-tags | Review and edit on unlocked cases | Review and edit on unlocked cases |
| Patterns | Review patterns and refresh scans | Review visible patterns |
| Account preferences | Own profile, security, notifications, and appearance; admins can also manage teammate notifications | Own profile, security, notifications, and appearance |

## Admin

Admins can see and manage the full organization.

Admins can:

- Invite admins or members and change team access.
- Manage facility locations and departments, and share public intake links, QR codes, and posters.
- Configure facility resolution approvals and required signers.
- View all active facilities in the organization.
- Change facility-level defaults such as each facility's grievance SLA.
- Change facility SLA defaults and manage teammate notification preferences.
- Refresh pattern scans.
- Delete open grievances. Resolved grievances cannot be deleted.
- Add case notes or attachments to resolved grievances without reopening them.

Facility profiles, facility activation, and billing are managed by Grievly rather than from the product app.

## Member

Members are scoped to assigned facilities.

Members can:

- See grievances, dashboard data, analytics, reports, and Assistant results for facilities they can access.
- Create and work grievances in their assigned facilities.
- Use My queue, Overdue, and High Priority views for accessible work.
- Add case notes, mentions, attachments, and resolution documentation where their access allows.
- Review and edit F-tags and categories on unlocked cases.
- View the active team directory and each teammate's department assignments.
- Invite new members into facilities they can access.
- Copy public intake links and download QR codes or posters for facilities they can access.
- Subscribe or unsubscribe from grievance notifications.
- Manage their own profile, security, notification preferences, and appearance.
- Reopen a resolved grievance they can access when more work is needed.

Members cannot change an existing user's access, change facility configuration, or refresh pattern scans. They also cannot remove existing users or delete grievances.

## Facility scope

Admins have access to all facilities in the organization.

Members only see facilities assigned to them. If a member cannot find a grievance, facility, report, pattern, or analytic result, first check their facility access.

## Workspace activation

Grievly manages organization creation, billing activation, and facility provisioning outside the product app. Until that work is complete, admins and members see the same **Workspace is not ready yet.** screen.

Contact your Grievly account manager if activation takes longer than expected. If you belong to multiple organizations, you can switch workspaces from the readiness screen.

## Least access

Use the narrowest access that lets each person do their job. Review access when a user changes role, moves facilities, leaves the organization, or no longer needs grievance log visibility.
