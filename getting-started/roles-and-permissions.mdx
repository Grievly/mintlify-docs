---
title: "Roles and permissions"
description: "Understand what administrators and members can see and manage in Grievly."
---

Grievly uses two workspace roles: **Admin** and **Member**.

Admins manage the workspace. Members work within the facilities they have been given access to.

## Role overview

| Area | Admin | Member |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Facility visibility | All active facilities in the organization | Assigned facilities only |
| Workspace setup | Complete BAA, billing, and first facility setup | Wait for admin setup |
| Team access | Invite, edit access, revoke invites, remove users | No team management |
| Facilities | Create, edit, archive, restore, and configure | View accessible facility context |
| Grievances | Work organization-wide grievances | Work accessible facility grievances |
| F-tags | Review and override | Review suggestions |
| Patterns | Review and manage pattern workflows | Review visible patterns |
| Settings | Workspace, compliance, billing, notifications | Own notification preferences |

## Admin

Admins can see and manage the full organization.

Admins can:

- Complete workspace setup, including the Business Associate Agreement, payment setup, and first facility.
- Invite teammates and change team access.
- Create, edit, archive, and restore facilities.
- Manage facility locations, departments, public intake links, and QR posters.
- View all active facilities in the organization.
- Change workspace defaults such as the default grievance SLA.
- View compliance and billing settings.
- Override F-tags.
- Manage pattern workflows, including scan controls, status changes, suppressions, and grievance exclusions.
- Reopen resolved grievances.

## Member

Members are scoped to assigned facilities.

Members can:

- See grievances, dashboard data, analytics, reports, and Assistant results for facilities they can access.
- Create and work grievances in their assigned facilities.
- Use My queue, Overdue, and High Priority views for accessible work.
- Add comments, mentions, attachments, and resolution documentation where their access allows.
- Subscribe or unsubscribe from grievance notifications.
- Manage their own notification preferences.

Members cannot manage workspace setup, billing, compliance settings, facility setup, team access, or admin-only pattern controls.

## Facility scope

Admins have access to all facilities in the organization.

Members only see facilities assigned to them. If a member cannot find a grievance, facility, report, pattern, or analytic result, first check their facility access.

## Waiting for admin setup

If setup is incomplete, admins see the setup step they need to finish. Members see a waiting screen asking them to contact an administrator.

This is expected when the workspace still needs a signed Business Associate Agreement, payment setup, or at least one active facility.

## Least access

Use the narrowest access that lets each person do their job. Review access when a user changes role, moves facilities, leaves the organization, or no longer needs grievance log visibility.
