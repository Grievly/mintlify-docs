The dashboard is the first place to review current grievance work.

It highlights operational signals, urgent queues, and work that may need attention.

## At a glance

| Area | What it helps you do |
| --- | --- |
| Needs attention | Open focused grievance lists for work that needs review |
| Metrics | Scan volume, open work, overdue work, and timeliness |
| Signals | Notice recurring categories, F-tags, or active patterns |
| Facility risk | Compare facility-level activity when available |

## What the dashboard shows

The dashboard can include:

- Key grievance metrics.
- Work needing attention.
- Overdue and due-soon grievances.
- Facility risk summaries.
- Category and F-tag signals.
- Active pattern summaries.

## Admin view

Admins can review organization-wide dashboard data.

Admins can filter the dashboard by facility when multiple active facilities are available.

Admins may also see portfolio-level facility risk information.

## Member view

Members see dashboard data for facilities they can access.

The member view emphasizes assigned work and accessible facility activity.

## Needs attention

Use **Needs attention** sections to open focused grievance lists, such as overdue work or cases due soon.

These sections are shortcuts into the grievance log. The grievance itself remains the source of truth.

## Signals

Signals help show where repeated categories, F-tags, or active patterns may need review.

Signals are for operational awareness. They do not replace case-level investigation or resolution documentation.
