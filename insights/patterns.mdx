title: "Patterns"
description: "Use the similarity map to review recurring themes and the grievances behind them."
---

Patterns are facility-specific groups of grievances that describe similar situations.

The Patterns map helps teams spot repeating concerns and possible emerging themes. It is advisory: proximity and grouping direct attention, but they do not prove causation, assign fault, close a grievance, or replace leadership review.

## Open a facility map

Open **Patterns** on a desktop computer.

- If you can access one facility, Grievly opens that map automatically.
- If you can access several facilities and none is selected, choose one when prompted.
- Use the facility menu in the upper-right to switch maps.

The Patterns facility menu changes only this map. It does not change the app-wide facility scope.

<Note>
  Facility maps are calculated independently. A dot's position in one facility cannot be compared with a position in another facility.
</Note>

## How to read the map

Every dot is one recent grievance. Grievances describing similar situations appear closer together.

| Map mark | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Shaded group | A repeating theme |
| Dotted group | A possible pattern forming from two near-identical reports |
| Neutral dot | A grievance that is not part of a repeating theme yet |
| Dot with a small hole | A grievance that belongs to more than one theme |

The exact position is not a score, and the grid has no axes to interpret. Use relative closeness and the shaded groups, then open the underlying cases before drawing a conclusion.

Select **How to read** on the map for the current legend and tips.

## Find and inspect grievances

Use map search to find an individual grievance by its number or title. Selecting a result centers its dot and opens a preview.

You can also select a dot directly. The preview lets you open the grievance record or open one of its patterns. When a grievance belongs to several patterns, choose which pattern to review.

## Open a pattern

Select a shaded group or its name to open the pattern details panel. Where groups overlap, Grievly asks which pattern you want.

The panel can include:

- A plain-language summary.
- Activity over time for the linked grievances.
- Up to three suggested next steps.
- A paginated list of linked grievances.

Select a linked grievance to open the case and confirm the underlying facts.

When you open a pattern from a grievance's right rail, the same detail panel opens over the case so you can review the pattern without first leaving the grievance.

## Map controls

Use the right-side controls to:

- Zoom in or out.
- Recenter the map.
- Show or hide all pattern names.

You can also pan the map and use a mouse wheel or trackpad to zoom.

## Processing and refreshes

The map uses recent facility activity. A very recent grievance may not appear until its analysis and positioning are ready, and the map reports when some grievances are not shown.

Automatic scans run daily and when new grievances arrive. Administrators can use **Refresh patterns** to queue another organization-wide scan. The map updates after the scan succeeds.

## Access and judgment

Members see patterns only for facilities they can access. Administrators can view all active facilities and refresh scans.

Always read the linked grievances and use the suggested next steps as review prompts. Staff remain responsible for investigation, follow-up, QAPI decisions, and resolution.
