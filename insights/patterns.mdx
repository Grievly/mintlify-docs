Patterns are clusters of similar grievances found by Grievly.

They help teams spot repeated concerns, shared signals, and possible operational issues inside the facilities they can access.

Patterns are advisory. They help direct attention, but they do not prove causation, assign fault, close grievances, or replace leadership review.

## Pattern anatomy

| Area | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| Feed card | Facility, last activity, momentum label, arrival sparkline, grievance count, and F-tag context |
| Arrival timeline | When linked grievances arrived over the detection window |
| Hotspots | Repeated location or shift signals, such as "5 of 7" grievances in one unit |
| Suggested next steps | Review prompts generated from the pattern |
| Linked grievances | The cases that make up the pattern |

## What a pattern contains

A pattern can include:

- Title and summary.
- Linked grievances.
- Facility context.
- Arrival sparkline over the recent detection window.
- Momentum label.
- Hotspots for repeated where or when signals.
- AI recommendations or fallback recommendations.
- Dominant F-tag when Grievly can identify one.

## Pattern feed

Open **Patterns** to review the feed.

The feed highlights the most recently active pattern first, then shows the rest as story cards. Each card shows:

- Facility name.
- Last seen date.
- Momentum label.
- Arrival sparkline.
- Number of linked grievances.
- F-tag or **Early signal** label when available.

## Momentum labels

Momentum labels describe how the arrivals are changing. They are based on the arrival sparkline, not a model confidence score.

| Label | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Picking up | More grievances arrived recently than in the prior period |
| New | The pattern started showing up in the last couple of months |
| Steady | Arrivals are holding about the same over time |
| Easing off | Fewer grievances arrived recently than in the prior period |

Use the label as a triage signal. Always open the linked grievances before deciding what action to take.

## Pattern detail

Open a pattern to see the full story.

The detail page includes:

- A plain-language summary.
- The arrival timeline for linked grievances.
- Repeated location or shift hotspots when they exist.
- Suggested next steps.
- The linked grievance list.

Select a linked grievance to open the case record and confirm the underlying facts.

## Admin scan control

Administrators can refresh the pattern scan from the Patterns page.

A refresh queues a background scan. When it completes, the feed updates with the latest pattern results.

## Member access

Members can review patterns that are visible within their facility scope.

## How to use patterns

1. Open **Patterns**.
2. Review the highlighted pattern and any additional story cards.
3. Use momentum and the sparkline to spot what needs attention.
4. Open a pattern to review hotspots, suggested next steps, and linked grievances.
5. Open linked grievances to confirm the underlying facts.
6. Use recommendations as review prompts, not final decisions.

## Advisory only

Patterns help direct attention. They do not prove causation, establish fault, close grievances, or replace QAPI or leadership review.
