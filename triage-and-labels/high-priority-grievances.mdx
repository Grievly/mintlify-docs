---
title: "High Priority grievances"
description: "Understand how High Priority is marked, where it appears, and what it does not mean."
---

High Priority marks an open grievance for faster operator attention.

It is an operational marker only. It is not a legal finding, state-reporting decision, citation, or official conclusion.

## At a glance

| Step | What happens |
| --- | --- |
| Intake | A grievance is created or submitted |
| Analysis | Grievly reviews the intake text for serious trigger language |
| Marker | If triggered, the grievance shows High Priority and a brief reason |
| Queue | Open High Priority grievances appear in the High Priority queue |
| Resolution | Resolved grievances drop out of the High Priority queue |

## How a grievance becomes High Priority

During AI intake analysis, Grievly looks for serious language that may need faster review.

Examples include:

- Alleged abuse.
- Neglect.
- Exploitation.
- Misappropriation of resident property.
- Injury of unknown source.
- Possible crime.
- Immediate safety risk.
- Repeated unresolved harm.
- Sharp decline or time-sensitive danger.

When the marker is set, Grievly stores a brief reason. The reason should name why the case needs faster review without repeating full resident details, dates, room numbers, or the full summary.

## What should not be High Priority

Routine service complaints, ordinary comfort issues, minor scheduling concerns, and resolved low-risk dissatisfaction should not be High Priority unless they also include a serious trigger.

## Where High Priority appears

High Priority can appear in:

- The sidebar **High Priority** queue.
- The grievance list.
- The grievance detail header.
- The case metadata rail.
- The AI summary timeline card.
- Report filters.

| Place | What it helps with |
| --- | --- |
| Sidebar queue | Find open High Priority work quickly |
| Grievance list | Compare High Priority cases with other filters |
| Detail header | Keep urgency visible while reviewing the case |
| Case metadata | Preserve the marker and reason near routing details |
| AI summary | Show why the marker was suggested |
| Reports | Export or review High Priority work |

## Queue behavior

The **High Priority** sidebar item opens the grievance list filtered to active High Priority work.

Resolved grievances drop out of the High Priority queue because the queue is meant for open work needing faster attention.

## How to work High Priority cases

1. Open the High Priority queue.
2. Review the reason and the full grievance record.
3. Confirm ownership and assignee.
4. Document immediate review steps in comments.
5. Add attachments or related grievance references when needed.
6. Resolve only after the required investigation, outcome, and communication are documented.

## Review carefully

High Priority can help surface urgent work, but staff still decide what action is required. Treat the marker as a prompt to review faster, not as proof that an allegation is true.
