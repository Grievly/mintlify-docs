---
title: "AI intake analysis"
description: "Understand Grievly's advisory intake summary, suggested labels, and retry behavior."
---

After a grievance is created, Grievly can analyze the intake record and suggest operational context.

AI intake analysis is advisory. It helps staff review and route work, but it does not replace human judgment.

## What AI can suggest

AI intake analysis may produce:

- A shorter case title.
- A triage summary.
- A suggested F-tag.
- A suggested category.
- A confidence indicator for the suggested label.
- A High Priority marker.

## Where suggestions appear

Suggestions appear on the grievance detail page in the timeline summary and case metadata.

If analysis is still running, the page may show loading states for the title or summary.

If analysis fails, Grievly shows a retry action on that grievance.

## Advisory only

AI can suggest or draft. It must not be treated as authoritative.

AI does not:

- Decide whether a grievance is legally substantiated.
- Make state-reporting decisions.
- Close a grievance.
- Replace required investigation or review.
- Replace staff review of the F-tag or category.

## Refreshes

When underlying case content changes, Grievly may refresh the current AI analysis. The page shows the current suggestion set; use the case timeline for the actual record of what staff changed and when.

## Good review practice

Use the AI output as a starting point. Confirm it against the actual grievance record, attachments, case notes, and facility context before relying on it in review or reporting.
