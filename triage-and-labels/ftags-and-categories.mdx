---
title: "F-tags and categories"
description: "Understand suggested regulatory labels, operational categories, and staff review."
---

Grievly tracks two kinds of labels on a grievance: F-tags and categories.

They are related but not the same.

## F-tags

An F-tag is a regulatory area connected to the grievance.

Grievly may suggest one F-tag during AI intake analysis. The suggestion helps staff review the likely regulatory area.

Any team member with access to the grievance can set or clear the F-tag while the grievance is open and not pending approval. Changes are recorded in timeline and audit activity.

## Categories

A category is an operational bucket used for filtering, reporting, analytics, and patterns.

Examples include care concerns, dietary issues, environment issues, dignity concerns, staffing concerns, and other broad operating themes.

Categories can be edited while the grievance is open and not pending approval.

## Confidence

AI suggestions may include a confidence indicator.

Use lower-confidence suggestions as a prompt for closer review. If the facts are thin, mixed, or ambiguous, confirm the label before relying on it for review or reporting.

## Manual review

AI suggestions are advisory.

Review the case details, resident words, reporter context, attachments, case notes, and facility operations before treating a label as final.

## Analytics, reports, and patterns

F-tags and categories support analytics, reports, and the descriptions attached to detected patterns. Pattern grouping itself is based on narrative similarity rather than matching labels.

Keep labels accurate enough for leadership review, QAPI, survey preparation, and trend analysis.
