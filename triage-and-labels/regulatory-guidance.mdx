title: "Regulatory guidance"
description: "Use CMS Appendix PP guidance, suggested next steps, and post-resolution compliance review on F-tagged grievances."
---

Grievly connects an F-tagged grievance to federal nursing-home guidance from CMS Appendix PP.

The same guidance supports three related workflows:

- Review the regulation and operational preparation prompts.
- Consider suggested next steps while the grievance is open.
- Review the documented resolution for possible gaps after the grievance is resolved.

<Warning>
  This feature covers federal Appendix PP guidance only. It does not determine compliance, provide legal advice, or account for state-specific reporting, deadlines, forms, or requirements. Staff remain responsible for investigation, decisions, and verification against current requirements.
</Warning>

## Open the F-tag guidance

On a grievance with available guidance, select the suggested F-tag badge or **View guidance** from the suggested-next-steps or compliance-review section.

The guidance window has three tabs:

| Tab | What it contains |
| --- | --- |
| Guidance | A plain-language summary, what surveyors may look for, common failure modes, and high-risk triggers |
| How to prepare | Quick checks, immediate fixes, records to audit, observations, interview prompts, and evidence to have ready |
| Source | The stored Appendix PP source text for the F-tag, plus a link to the CMS nursing-home guidance page |

The **Guidance** and **How to prepare** tabs are AI-generated from the federal source and are labeled as operational references. Use the **Source** tab and current CMS material to verify the underlying language.

## Suggested next steps for an open grievance

When an open grievance has an F-tag, Grievly automatically prepares advisory next steps the first time the case is viewed.

The **Suggested next steps** row appears collapsed in the AI summary. Expand it to review the list or open the connected guidance.

The suggestions use the grievance record and the selected F-tag's guidance. They do not update fields, assign work, send notifications, or move the grievance toward resolution. If the F-tag changes, Grievly prepares a new set against the replacement tag.

## Compliance review after resolution

When an F-tagged grievance is resolved, Grievly automatically compares the documented resolution with the selected tag's federal guidance the first time the resolved case is viewed.

The collapsed **Compliance review** keeps one of these advisory results visible in its header:

- **Aligned**
- **Minor gaps**
- **Significant gaps**

Expand the review to read the assessment and any documentation or action gaps to consider. The review is saved with the grievance so later viewers see the same snapshot.

The review does not reopen the grievance, change the resolution, decide compliance, or make a reporting decision. Reopen the grievance through the normal workflow if staff decide that more work is required.

## When each item appears

| Case state | Guidance | Suggested next steps | Compliance review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Open with an F-tag | Available | Generated automatically | Not shown |
| Pending resolution approval with an F-tag | Available | Not shown | Not shown |
| Resolved with an F-tag | Available | Not shown | Generated automatically |
| No F-tag | Not available | Not shown | Not shown |

If a newly selected F-tag does not yet have guidance available, the guidance-based items may not appear.

## Ask Assistant about federal guidance

Assistant can answer questions tied to a known F-tag or search Appendix PP for questions that span several tags or use everyday language. F-tag citations in an answer open the same guidance window.
