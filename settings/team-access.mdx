---
title: "Manage team access"
description: "Invite teammates and manage access to organizations and facilities."
---

Team access controls who can see and manage information in Grievly. Use the narrowest access that still lets each person do their job.

Administrators can invite users, change roles, change facility scope, revoke pending invites, or remove users. Members can use Team as a read-only directory.

For role definitions, see [Roles and permissions](/getting-started/roles-and-permissions).

## Invite a teammate

1. Open **Team**.
2. Select **Invite member**.
3. Enter the person's email address.
4. Choose their role and access.
5. Send the invite.

Clerk sends the invitation. The invitee enters their name during signup.

## Choose a role

Choose **Admin** when the user needs organization-wide setup or management access.

Choose **Member** when the user should work only in assigned facilities.

Admins ignore facility scope because they can access all facilities. Members must have at least one facility selected before an invite can be sent.

## Update access

Use team settings to adjust facility access or role permissions when responsibilities change.

Review access when a user changes role, moves facilities, leaves the organization, or no longer needs grievance log visibility.

Administrators cannot change their own role or facility scope. Ask another admin to make those changes.

## Team directory

Open **Team** to view people in the workspace.

Administrators can see active users and pending invitations. Members see active teammates in their accessible facility scope.

The team table can show:

- Name and email.
- Facility access, for administrators.
- Department assignments.
- Role, for administrators.
- Invite status, for administrators.

Select an active teammate to open the detail page. Invited users stay in the table until they accept the invite or an administrator revokes it.

## Filter the team table

Use search to find a teammate by name or email.

Administrators can also filter by:

- Facility.
- Role.
- Status, including Active and Invited.

Team filters are saved in the page URL, so refresh, back/forward navigation, and shared links keep the same view. Use **Reset filters** to clear the current search and filters.

## Departments

The team table includes a **Departments** column. One department is shown directly; multiple departments collapse into a count that can be hovered to see the list.

A team member detail page also shows departments assigned to that person, grouped by facility.

Departments are managed from the facility's **Departments** tab. Department assignment can affect routing and notifications.

## Invited users

Invited users appear in the team table with an **Invited** badge until they accept the invitation and sign in.

Administrators can revoke a pending invite. Revoking an invite removes the pending user row and stops the invitation link from working.

## Remove a user

Deleting a user removes their access to the workspace.

Past grievances they created, were assigned to, or participated in still show their name for audit history.

## Access troubleshooting

If a user cannot see the right grievances, confirm:

- They are in the correct organization.
- They have access to the correct facility.
- Their role allows the action they are trying to take.
- The facility is active.
- The grievance is not hidden by filters or date range.
