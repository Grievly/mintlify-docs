---
title: "Privacy and PHI basics"
description: "Handle resident and grievance information carefully while using Grievly."
---

Grievly is used for sensitive grievance operations. Users should document enough to support review and resolution, but avoid unnecessary sensitive information.

## Treat as sensitive

Treat the following as sensitive:

- Resident names.
- Room numbers and unit details.
- Grievance narratives.
- Resident words.
- Reporter contact information.
- Audio, images, documents, attachments, or other supporting files.
- Attachment contents.
- Case comments and investigation notes.

## Keep the full record in Grievly

Do not move PHI into unapproved systems.

Avoid copying grievance narratives, resident identifiers, attachments, or case comments into:

- Support emails unless specifically approved.
- External chat tools.
- Analytics tools.
- Issue trackers.
- Shared documents that are not approved for PHI.

## Notifications and emails

Notification emails are intentionally brief. They identify the grievance, facility, action, and a link back to Grievly.

Open Grievly to review the full case record.

## Reports

Reports may contain sensitive resident, reporter, and case information.

Before sharing a report, confirm:

- The facility scope is correct.
- The date range is correct.
- The status and label filters are correct.
- Attachments are included only when needed.
- Recipients are approved to receive the information.

## AI outputs

AI summaries, Assistant answers, and pattern recommendations may reference grievance information.

Use them inside Grievly as review aids. Do not treat them as final decisions or copy sensitive content into unapproved tools.
