This page defines the filters users see most often in CareResolve.

## Statuses

### Open

Open grievances are active cases that have not been resolved.

### Resolved

Resolved grievances have a completed resolution record.

Resolved cases are read-only in most areas. Any user who can access a case can reopen it when more work is needed. Reopening requires a reason and preserves the original due date and history.

The resolved list is ordered by resolution finalization time by default. This is the system-recorded time the grievance actually became resolved, which is separate from the operator-entered **Outcome date** in the resolution packet.

### Pending approval

Pending approval means the grievance has been submitted for resolution approval and required signers have not all signed yet.

Pending approval is not a saved grievance status. The grievance remains open and unresolved until the approval workflow completes.

## Derived queues

Derived queues are calculated from grievance data. They are not separate saved statuses.

### Overdue

An open grievance is overdue when its due date is in the past.

Resolved grievances do not appear as overdue.

### High Priority

An open grievance is High Priority when it has been marked for faster operator attention.

Resolved High Priority grievances no longer appear in the High Priority queue.

### My queue

My queue shows grievances assigned to you.

It also shows grievances waiting for your resolution approval when you are a required signer.

## Open and resolved list modes

The grievance list opens in open-only mode. Use **Show resolved grievances** to switch to resolved records; the switch does not combine open and resolved rows.

## Common list filters

The grievance list can filter by:

- Facility scope from the app-wide switcher.
- Status: Pending Approval, Overdue, or High Priority.
- Category.
- F-tag.
- Assignee.
- Received date range.
- Search text.
- Has attachments.
- Public submission.
- Resident Council submission.
- Employee involved.
- Third party concern.
- Anonymous reporter.

Open is the default mode and Resolved uses the separate switch. In resolved mode, the **Status** filter can narrow records by High Priority or by the advisory compliance-review results Aligned, Minor gaps, and Significant gaps.

The Resident Council filter matches both source-linked grievances and older or intentionally unlinked Council submissions.

## Search

Search can match grievance identifiers, titles, resident and involved-person information, concern text, case notes, attachment information, resolution fields, and timeline activity.

If search results look too narrow, clear filters first and then search again.

The top bar search also supports recently viewed grievances, result previews, and filters for received date, facility, assignee, status, category, F-tag, and other grievance facets.

## Sorting

The open grievance list defaults to Created, newest first. The resolved list defaults to Resolved, newest finalization first. Sortable headers include ID, Received, Due or Resolved depending on the current mode, and Created.

Sorting changes the order of results. It does not change which records match the current filters.

## Access scope

Filters only operate inside the data you can access.

Admins filter across the organization. Members filter within assigned facilities.
